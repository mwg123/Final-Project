Not Rated (Probable PG for Action Violence)

Review

I remember seeing a few bits of "Dragonball Z" on TV growing up and thinking it was extremely stupid. So why did I spend more money than usual to see a spin-off movie of the show in the theater? I'm not sure, really. A chance to dish out some bile? Or perhaps to see if, now that I've seen some good anime (like "Inuyasha" or anything) by Hayao Miyazaki, it wasn't as bad as I remember (the latter is why I subjected myself to watching "The Royal Tenenbaums" a second time). If nothing else, it could give me another entry for my Bottom 10 list this year.

So what's the verdict? Look at the rating and take a guess.

"Dragonball Z: Resurrection "F" is for fans only. Anyone who hasn't grown to love the characters while watching the show will find little of any interest here. Granted, "Inuyasha: Affections Touching Across Time" wasn't the most friendly to people who weren't already fans of the show, but it at least gave a perfunctory set-up. That doesn't happen here, where there is no set-up or character development whatsoever.
